History

Futura was designed by renowned German type designer Paul Renner in 1927. It was commissioned as a typeface by Bauer Type Foundry for the New Frankfurt project, which was an affordable modernist housing project. The typeface was based off of the geometric Bauhaus design style that had its boom between 1919 and 1933. The Bauhaus ideology was based on simple, modern, and functional geometry. Function over form resulted in a typeface that didnt include unnecessary ornamental elements.

Renner wasn't invovled with the Bauhuas but did share the same ideology of stripping forms down to their essence. The Futura font overall resulted in clean, simple shapes that conveyed a modern and futuristic idea. It became instantly popular upon its realease, and now seen about everywhere.
